Exercise 10 Solution

Given:

  • Deceleration: \( a = -6.5 \, \text{m/s}^2 \)
  • Distance covered: \( s = 45 \, \text{m} \)
  • Final velocity: \( v = 0 \, \text{m/s} \) (since the car stops)

We need to calculate:

  1. (a) Time taken for braking, \( t \)
  2. (b) Initial velocity, \( v_0 \)

Solution:

(a) Time Taken for Braking

We use the equation of motion:

\[ v = v_0 + at \]

Rearranging for \( t \):

\[ t = \frac{v - v_0}{a} \]

Since \( v = 0 \):

\[ t = \frac{-v_0}{a} \]

We first need to find \( v_0 \) using the second equation of motion.

(b) Initial Velocity

We use the equation:

\[ v^2 = v_0^2 + 2as \]

Rearranging for \( v_0^2 \):

\[ v_0^2 = v^2 - 2as \]

Substitute \( v = 0 \):

\[ v_0^2 = -2as \]

Substitute \( a = -6.5 \, \text{m/s}^2 \) and \( s = 45 \, \text{m} \):

\[ v_0^2 = -2(-6.5)(45) \] \[ v_0^2 = 585 \]

Taking the square root:

\[ v_0 = \sqrt{585} \approx 24.2 \, \text{m/s} \]

Time Calculation

Now substitute \( v_0 \) into the equation for \( t \):

\[ t = \frac{-v_0}{a} \]

Substitute \( v_0 = 24.2 \, \text{m/s} \) and \( a = -6.5 \, \text{m/s}^2 \):

\[ t = \frac{-24.2}{-6.5} \approx 3.72 \, \text{seconds} \]

Final Results:

(a) Time taken for braking: \( t \approx 3.72 \, \text{seconds} \)

(b) Initial velocity: \( v_0 \approx 24.2 \, \text{m/s} \)
