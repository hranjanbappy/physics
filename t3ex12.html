Exercise 12 Solution

(a) Plot the time-acceleration, time-velocity, and time-distance diagrams

The motion of the suitcase can be divided into three phases:

  1. Accelerated motion for the first 2 seconds with acceleration \(a = 1 \, \mathrm{m/s^2}\).
  2. Constant velocity motion for the next 2 seconds.
  3. Accelerated motion for the final second with acceleration \(a = -2 \, \mathrm{m/s^2}\).

The time-acceleration diagram is as follows:

\[ a(t) = \begin{cases} 1 \, \mathrm{m/s^2} & \text{for } 0 \le t \le 2 \, \mathrm{s} \\ 0 & \text{for } 2 < t \le 4 \, \mathrm{s} \\ -2 \, \mathrm{m/s^2} & \text{for } 4 < t \le 5 \, \mathrm{s} \end{cases} \]

The time-velocity diagram is:

\[ v(t) = \begin{cases} v_0 + at & \text{for } 0 \le t \le 2 \, \mathrm{s} \\ v_2 & \text{for } 2 < t \le 4 \, \mathrm{s} \\ v_2 + a(t-4) & \text{for } 4 < t \le 5 \, \mathrm{s} \end{cases} \]

The time-distance diagram is:

\[ x(t) = \begin{cases} v_0 t + \frac{1}{2} at^2 & \text{for } 0 \le t \le 2 \, \mathrm{s} \\ v_2 t + x_2 & \text{for } 2 < t \le 4 \, \mathrm{s} \\ v_2 (t-4) + \frac{1}{2} a (t-4)^2 + x_4 & \text{for } 4 < t \le 5 \, \mathrm{s} \end{cases} \]

(b) What is the maximal speed?

The maximal speed occurs at the end of the first 2 seconds, when the acceleration is \(a = 1 \, \mathrm{m/s^2}\) and the initial velocity is \(v_0 = 0 \, \mathrm{m/s}\).

\[ v_2 = v_0 + at = 0 + 1 \cdot 2 = 2 \, \mathrm{m/s} \]

Therefore, the maximal speed is \(2 \, \mathrm{m/s}\).

(c) What is the velocity of the suitcase after 5 s?

After the first 4 seconds, the suitcase moves with constant velocity \(v_2 = 2 \, \mathrm{m/s}\). In the final second, the suitcase is accelerated with \(a = -2 \, \mathrm{m/s^2}\).

\[ v(5) = v_2 + a(5-4) = 2 + (-2) \cdot 1 = 0 \, \mathrm{m/s} \]

Therefore, the velocity of the suitcase after 5 seconds is \(0 \, \mathrm{m/s}\).

(d) What is the total distance covered by the suitcase?

To calculate the total distance, we need to sum the distances covered in each phase of the motion.

  1. Distance covered in the first 2 seconds: \[ x_2 = v_0 t + \frac{1}{2} at^2 = 0 \cdot 2 + \frac{1}{2} \cdot 1 \cdot 2^2 = 2 \, \mathrm{m} \]
  2. Distance covered in the next 2 seconds: \[ x_4 - x_2 = v_2 (4-2) = 2 \cdot 2 = 4 \, \mathrm{m} \]
  3. Distance covered in the final second: \[ x_5 - x_4 = v_2 (1) + \frac{1}{2} a (1)^2 = 2 \cdot 1 + \frac{1}{2} \cdot (-2) \cdot 1^2 = 1 \, \mathrm{m} \]

The total distance covered by the suitcase is the sum of these: \[ x_5 = x_2 + (x_4 - x_2) + (x_5 - x_4) = 2 + 4 + 1 = 7 \, \mathrm{m} \]

Therefore, the total distance covered by the suitcase is \(7 \, \mathrm{m}\).
