Exercise 11

Standing on top of a \(10 \, \text{m}\) high tower, you throw a ball upwards. The ball leaves your hand with a perpendicular initial velocity of \(v_y = v_0 = 5 \, \text{m/s}\).

(a) What is the maximal throwing height of the ball (referred to the ground)?

(b) After which time does the ball reach the ground at the foot of the tower?

(c) How much is the velocity with which the ball impinges on the ground?

(d) Plot the corresponding time-distance and time-velocity-diagram for the specified scenario.

Solution

(a) Maximal Throwing Height

At maximum height, the velocity becomes zero (\(v_y = 0\)). Using the equation:

\[ v_y^2 = v_0^2 - 2 g h_{\text{max}} \]

Rearranging for \(h_{\text{max}}\):

\[ h_{\text{max}} = \frac{v_0^2}{2 g} \]

Substituting \(v_0 = 5 \, \text{m/s}\) and \(g = 9.8 \, \text{m/s}^2\):

\[ h_{\text{max}} = \frac{5^2}{2 \cdot 9.8} = \frac{25}{19.6} \approx 1.28 \, \text{m} \]

Total height above the ground:

\[ H_{\text{total}} = h_{\text{max}} + h = 1.28 + 10 = 11.28 \, \text{m} \]

(b) Time to Reach the Ground

**Step 1:** Time to reach maximum height:

\[ t_{\text{up}} = \frac{v_0}{g} = \frac{5}{9.8} \approx 0.51 \, \text{s} \]

**Step 2:** Time to fall from the maximum height:

\[ t_{\text{down}} = \sqrt{\frac{2 H_{\text{total}}}{g}} \] \[ t_{\text{down}} = \sqrt{\frac{2 \cdot 11.28}{9.8}} \approx \sqrt{2.3} \approx 1.52 \, \text{s} \]

Total time:

\[ t_{\text{total}} = t_{\text{up}} + t_{\text{down}} = 0.51 + 1.52 \approx 2.03 \, \text{s} \]

(c) Velocity on Impact

Using the equation:

\[ v_y^2 = v_0^2 + 2 g h \]

Substituting \(v_0 = 5 \, \text{m/s}\), \(g = 9.8 \, \text{m/s}^2\), and \(h = 11.28 \, \text{m}\):

\[ v_y^2 = 0 + 2 \cdot 9.8 \cdot 11.28 = 221.57 \] \[ v_y = \sqrt{221.57} \approx 14.88 \, \text{m/s} \]

The velocity on impact is directed downward, so \(v_y = -14.88 \, \text{m/s}\).

(d) Diagrams

The time-distance and time-velocity diagrams can be plotted as follows:

  • **Time-Distance Diagram:** A parabola showing the ball rising and falling, with a maximum height at \(t_{\text{up}}\).
  • **Time-Velocity Diagram:** A straight line with negative slope showing the velocity decreasing to zero at \(t_{\text{up}}\) and increasing negatively thereafter.
