Solution to Atwood's Machine Problem

Given:

  • Masses: \( M \) and \( M + m \)
  • Frictionless pulley system

(a) Acceleration acting on the weight pieces:

The forces acting on the system are:

  • Gravitational force on \( M \): \( F_M = Mg \)
  • Gravitational force on \( M + m \): \( F_{M+m} = (M + m)g \)

Let the acceleration of the system be \( a \). Using Newton's second law:

For \( M \): \( T - Mg = Ma \)
For \( M + m \): \( (M + m)g - T = (M + m)a \)

Adding the two equations eliminates \( T \):

\[ (M + m)g - Mg = (M + m)a + Ma \]

Simplify:

\[ mg = (2M + m)a \]

Solve for \( a \):

\[ a = \frac{mg}{2M + m} \]

(b) Force pulling on the rope (Tension \( T \)):

Using the equation for \( M \):

\[ T - Mg = Ma \]

Substitute \( a = \frac{mg}{2M + m} \):

\[ T = Mg + M\left(\frac{mg}{2M + m}\right) \]

Simplify:

\[ T = Mg \left(1 + \frac{m}{2M + m}\right) \]

Final Results:

  • Acceleration:
    \[ a = \frac{mg}{2M + m} \]
  • Tension:
    \[ T = Mg \left(1 + \frac{m}{2M + m}\right) \]
