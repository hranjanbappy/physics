Physics Solution

Motion Under Time-Dependent Force

Given:
  • Mass: \( m = 2 \, \text{kg} \)
  • Force: \( F_x = b \cdot t \) with \( b = 20 \, \text{N/s} \)
  • Initial position: \( x_0 = 5 \, \text{m} \)
  • Initial velocity: \( v_x(t = 0) = 0 \, \text{m/s} \)
  • Initial time: \( t_0 = 0 \, \text{s} \)

1. Acceleration:

Using Newton's second law of motion: \[ F_x = m \cdot a_x \] Substitute \( F_x = b \cdot t \): \[ a_x = \frac{F_x}{m} = \frac{b \cdot t}{m} \] Substituting \( b = 20 \, \text{N/s}, m = 2 \, \text{kg} \): \[ a_x = \frac{20 \cdot t}{2} = 10 \cdot t \, \text{m/s}^2 \] At \( t = 2 \, \text{s} \): \[ a_x(2) = 10 \cdot 2 = 20 \, \text{m/s}^2 \]

2. Velocity:

Acceleration is the rate of change of velocity: \[ a_x = \frac{d v_x}{d t} \] Integrate to find \( v_x \): \[ v_x(t) = \int a_x \, dt = \int (10 \cdot t) \, dt = 5 \cdot t^2 + C \] At \( t = 0, v_x = 0 \), so \( C = 0 \): \[ v_x(t) = 5 \cdot t^2 \] At \( t = 2 \, \text{s} \): \[ v_x(2) = 5 \cdot (2^2) = 5 \cdot 4 = 20 \, \text{m/s} \]

3. Distance (Position):

Velocity is the rate of change of position: \[ v_x = \frac{dx}{dt} \] Integrate to find \( x(t) \): \[ x(t) = \int v_x \, dt = \int (5 \cdot t^2) \, dt = \frac{5}{3} \cdot t^3 + C_1 \] At \( t = 0, x = x_0 = 5 \), so \( C_1 = 5 \): \[ x(t) = \frac{5}{3} \cdot t^3 + 5 \] At \( t = 2 \, \text{s} \): \[ x(2) = \frac{5}{3} \cdot (2^3) + 5 = \frac{5}{3} \cdot 8 + 5 = \frac{40}{3} + 5 = \frac{55}{3} \approx 18.33 \, \text{m} \]

Final Results:

  • Acceleration at \( t = 2 \, \text{s} \): \( a_x = 20 \, \text{m/s}^2 \)
  • Velocity at \( t = 2 \, \text{s} \): \( v_x = 20 \, \text{m/s} \)
  • Distance at \( t = 2 \, \text{s} \): \( x = 18.33 \, \text{m} \)
