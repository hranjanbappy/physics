Time-Dependent Distance Between Falling Tennis Balls

Derivation

To derive the time-dependent distance between two tennis balls when the second is released with a delay \( t_{\text{delay}} \), we analyze their motion as follows:

1. Equations of Motion

For an object in free fall, the distance fallen at time \( t \) is given by:

\[ y = \frac{1}{2} g t^2, \] where:
  • \( y \) is the distance fallen,
  • \( g = 9.81 \, \text{m/s}^2 \) is the acceleration due to gravity,
  • \( t \) is the time elapsed since release.

2. Motion of Each Ball

First Ball: The first ball starts falling at \( t = 0 \). Its position at time \( t \) is:

\[ y_1(t) = \frac{1}{2} g t^2. \]

Second Ball: The second ball starts falling after a delay of \( t_{\text{delay}} \). Its position at time \( t \) is:

  • If \( t < t_{\text{delay}} \): It hasn’t started falling yet, so:
    • \[ y_2(t) = 0. \]
  • If \( t \geq t_{\text{delay}} \): It falls for \( t - t_{\text{delay}} \) seconds, so:
    • \[ y_2(t) = \frac{1}{2} g (t - t_{\text{delay}})^2. \]

3. Time-Dependent Distance Between the Balls

The distance between the two balls, \( d(t) \), is:

\[ d(t) = \lvert y_1(t) - y_2(t) \rvert. \]

Substitute \( y_1(t) \) and \( y_2(t) \):

Case 1: \( t < t_{\text{delay}} \)

The second ball hasn’t started falling, so:

\[ d(t) = y_1(t) = \frac{1}{2} g t^2. \] Case 2: \( t \geq t_{\text{delay}} \)

Both balls are falling, so:

\[ d(t) = \lvert \frac{1}{2} g t^2 - \frac{1}{2} g (t - t_{\text{delay}})^2 \rvert. \]

Simplify:

\[ d(t) = \frac{1}{2} g \left[ t^2 - (t - t_{\text{delay}})^2 \right]. \]

Expand \( (t - t_{\text{delay}})^2 \):

\[ (t - t_{\text{delay}})^2 = t^2 - 2t t_{\text{delay}} + t_{\text{delay}}^2. \]

Substitute back:

\[ d(t) = \frac{1}{2} g \left[ t^2 - (t^2 - 2t t_{\text{delay}} + t_{\text{delay}}^2) \right]. \]

Simplify further:

\[ d(t) = \frac{1}{2} g \left[ 2t t_{\text{delay}} - t_{\text{delay}}^2 \right]. \]

Final Expression

For \( t \geq t_{\text{delay}} \):

\[ d(t) = g t_{\text{delay}} t - \frac{1}{2} g t_{\text{delay}}^2. \]

Summary

\[ d(t) = \begin{cases} \frac{1}{2} g t^2, & \text{if } t < t_{\text{delay}}, \\ g t_{\text{delay}} t - \frac{1}{2} g t_{\text{delay}}^2, & \text{if } t \geq t_{\text{delay}}. \end{cases} \]
